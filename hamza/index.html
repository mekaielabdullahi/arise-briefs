What an AIOS Actually Is

"AIOS" stands for AI Operating System — and it's more than a chatbot. A chatbot answers questions. An operating system runs things. The difference comes down to four layers, often called the Four C's: Context, Connections, Capabilities, and Cadence. Each one builds on the one before it.

C
Context
What it knows

Everything the AI understands about your firm — your deals, portfolio, investors, strategy, and how you like things done. This is the foundation. Skip it and you get generic answers; nail it and the AI responds like someone who's worked here for years.

C
Connections
What it plugs into

The systems where your work already lives — email, calendar, drive, CRM, your deal and portfolio data. Connections let the AI pull real, current information instead of guessing or waiting for you to paste it in.

C
Capabilities
What it can do

The actions it can take on your behalf — draft and send an email, update a record, generate a report or chart, kick off a multi-step workflow. This is where it stops talking and starts doing the work.

C
Cadence
When it acts

The schedule. Some things you ask for; others should just happen — a 7am daily brief, an alert when a new deal comes in, a report every Friday. Cadence is what makes the system proactive instead of waiting to be asked.

Build order matters: Context first, then Connections and Capabilities, then Cadence — you never automate a workflow until it works by hand. That's exactly why this rolls out in steps. The AIOS Install & Setup lays the Context and your first Connections; the AI Audit then maps which Capabilities and Cadences are worth building next.
